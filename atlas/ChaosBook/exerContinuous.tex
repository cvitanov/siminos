\exercise{Classify possible symmetries of solutions for your research
problem.} { \label{exer:fludProbsSymms}
Classify types of solutions you expect in your research problem by
their symmetries. Literature examples:
\pCf\rf{HGC08},
pipe flow (sect.~2.2 and appendix A of \refref{ACHKW11}),
\KS\ (see symmetry discussions of \refref{Christiansen97,SCD07}, and
     probably many better papers out there that we are less familiar with),
Euclidean symmetries of doubly-periodic 2D models of cardiac tissue,
2D Kolmogorov flow\rf{ChaKer12},
\twomode\ flow (Dangelmayr\rf{Dang86};
    Armbruster, Guckenheimer and Holmes\rf{AGHO288};
    Jones and Proctor\rf{JoPro87}; Porter and Knobloch\rf{PoKno05};
    Golubitsky \etal\rf{golubII}, Sect. XX.1),
2D ABC flow\rf{DFGHMS86};
perturbed {Coulomb} systems\rf{HW90};
systems with discrete symmetries\rf{Laur91,BaVo86};
ChaosBook %\refexam{dscr:ReflectA}
reflection symmetric $1d$ map;
%\refexam{exmp:HamHenonMap}
Hamiltonian H\'enon map;
Hamiltonian Lozi map,
\etc.
    } %end {exer:fludProbsSymms}
